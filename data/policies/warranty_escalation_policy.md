Section 1: Product Warranty Policy
1.1 Manufacturer's Warranty
Most ShopCore products come with manufacturer warranties:

Electronics: Typically 1 year from purchase date
Appliances: Typically 1–2 years from purchase date
Clothing and accessories: No manufacturer warranty (covered by ShopCore return policy)
Tools and hardware: Varies by brand (1–5 years typical)

Manufacturer warranty claims are handled by the manufacturer, not ShopCore. ShopCore can provide contact information but does not process manufacturer warranty claims.
1.2 ShopCore Extended Warranty
ShopCore-brand extended warranties (ShopCare Plans):

Available for electronics and appliances over $100
Coverage: 1, 2, or 3 years beyond manufacturer warranty
Coverage includes: Mechanical breakdown, accidental damage (Plus tier only)
Does NOT cover: Physical damage from misuse, water damage (Standard tier), normal wear and tear

1.3 ShopCare Claim Process
To file a ShopCare claim:

Contact ShopCare support (separate from standard customer support)
Describe the defect or damage
Provide purchase proof and ShopCare plan number
Item may be sent to certified repair center
If unrepairable: Replacement or refund at ShopCore's discretion


Section 2: Customer Complaint Escalation
2.1 Escalation Triggers
Agent must escalate a ticket to a supervisor or specialist team when:

Resolution requires exception to documented policy
Customer claims a regional/legal right not addressed in current policy documentation
Fraud or account security concerns are raised
Ticket involves a claim over $500
Customer requests to speak with a supervisor
Agent is uncertain about applicable policy

2.2 Escalation Path
Standard escalation path:

Tier 1 Agent → Tier 2 Specialist (general escalations, up to $250)
Tier 2 Specialist → Team Lead (claims $250–$500, policy exceptions)
Team Lead → Department Manager (claims over $500, legal concerns)
Department Manager → Legal/Compliance (regional law, fraud, liability)

2.3 Customer Notification During Escalation
When escalating:

Inform customer of escalation (do not explain internal process in detail)
Provide expected response timeframe: Tier 2 response within 24 hours; Manager within 48 hours
Issue interim acknowledgment: "We are reviewing your case and will respond by [date]"

2.4 Escalation Timeframes

Tier 1 to Tier 2: Same business day
Tier 2 resolution: Within 2 business days
Manager escalations: Within 3 business days
Legal/Compliance: No guaranteed timeline; customer notified of delay


Section 3: Service Recovery Standards
3.1 Goodwill Gestures
Agents may offer goodwill gestures for poor customer experiences:

Store credit up to $15: Agent discretion (no approval needed)
Store credit $15–$50: Tier 1 supervisor approval required
Store credit over $50: Tier 2 approval required
Coupon codes: Agents may issue up to 20% off coupons for next purchase

3.2 Goodwill Gesture Limits

Maximum of two goodwill gestures per customer per calendar year
Goodwill gestures are non-transferable and apply to one order only
Agents cannot override stated policy using goodwill gestures (goodwill is a supplement, not a policy bypass)

3.3 Customer Retention Offers
Customer retention offers (designed to prevent cancellations/returns) may include:

Subscription discount for 1–3 months
Waived return shipping fee (one-time)
Early access to sales or promotions
These offers require Team Lead approval.


Section 4: Legal & Compliance Escalation
4.1 When to Involve Legal
Legal/Compliance team must be notified for:

Any customer claiming to be a lawyer or threatening legal action
Media inquiries about disputes
Regulatory complaints (BBB, FTC, state attorney general)
Data breach or privacy violation reports
Class action related inquiries

4.2 Agent Communication During Legal Escalation
When legal concerns arise:

Agents must not make any admissions of liability
Agents must not make promises of specific outcomes
Direct customer to: "Our legal team will be in touch regarding your inquiry"
Document all communications thoroughly


Section 5: Complaint Documentation Requirements
5.1 Required Documentation
All escalated cases must include:

Summary of customer complaint
Order details (number, items, dates, amounts)
Actions already taken by Tier 1
Policy sections cited and applied
Reason for escalation
Customer contact history

5.2 Prohibited in Documentation
Documentation must never include:

Agent's personal opinions about the customer
Unverified allegations
Discriminatory language or observations
Speculative conclusions about fraud without evidence
