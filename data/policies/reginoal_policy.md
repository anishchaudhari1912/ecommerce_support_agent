Section 1: California (CA) Consumer Protections
1.1 California Return Rights (CA Civil Code §1723)
Under California law, retailers must conspicuously post their return policy. ShopCore's standard 30-day return policy meets and exceeds California minimum requirements. However:

If no return policy is posted at point of sale, California consumers are entitled to a 30-day return right by law regardless
California consumers may not be charged restocking fees greater than 10% of the purchase price for non-defective item returns

1.2 California Final Sale Disclosure
Items marked "Final Sale" sold to California consumers must have the Final Sale designation clearly displayed:

On the product page
In the shopping cart
At checkout confirmation
Failure to display in all three locations entitles the CA consumer to standard return rights regardless of "Final Sale" marking.

1.3 Automatic Renewal (CA BPC §17601)
Subscription services sold to California consumers must:

Clearly disclose auto-renewal terms before purchase
Send renewal reminders 15–45 days before annual renewal
Allow cancellation via the same mechanism used to subscribe


Section 2: European Union (EU) Consumer Rights
2.1 EU 14-Day Right of Withdrawal
Under EU Directive 2011/83/EU (Consumer Rights Directive), EU customers have a 14-day right of withdrawal from the date of receipt — no reason required.

Applicable to all distance selling (online purchases)
Customer pays return shipping unless seller is at fault
Refund must be issued within 14 days of return receipt

2.2 EU Exceptions to Right of Withdrawal
The EU 14-day withdrawal right does not apply to:

Sealed goods that are not suitable for return due to health or hygiene reasons if unsealed after delivery (hygiene items)
Perishable goods
Digital content if download has begun (with consumer consent)
Customized/personalized goods
Goods mixed inseparably with other goods after delivery

2.3 EU Legal Guarantee
EU consumers are entitled to a 2-year legal guarantee on all products:

Defects existing at time of delivery must be repaired or replaced free of charge
For defects appearing in the first 12 months, defect is presumed to have existed at delivery
ShopCore's warranty policy is in addition to, not replacement of, EU legal guarantee


Section 3: United Kingdom (UK) Consumer Rights
3.1 UK Consumer Rights Act 2015
UK customers are protected by the Consumer Rights Act 2015:

30-day right to reject defective goods for full refund
6-month repair/replacement rights for defective goods after 30 days
6-year claim period for goods not lasting a reasonable time

3.2 UK Distance Selling Regulations
UK online purchases include a 14-day cooling-off period (Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013):

Starts from day of delivery
No reason required; customer pays return shipping
Refund within 14 days of return

3.3 UK Final Sale
"Final Sale" designations are valid in the UK provided they are clearly communicated. However, defective goods retain statutory rights regardless of "Final Sale" status.

Section 4: Canada Consumer Protections
4.1 Province-Specific Rules
Canadian consumer protection laws vary by province:
Quebec (Consumer Protection Act):

30-day right to cancel distance contracts
Merchants cannot require reason for return within 30 days
Refund must be issued within 15 days of return

Ontario (Consumer Protection Act, 2002):

7-day cooling-off period for internet agreements over $50
Full refund within 15 days

British Columbia (Business Practices and Consumer Protection Act):

Similar 7-day cooling-off provision

Other Provinces: ShopCore's standard 30-day policy applies and meets or exceeds most provincial minimum requirements.

Section 5: Conflict Resolution — Regional vs. Standard Policy
5.1 Hierarchy of Policy Application
When standard ShopCore policy conflicts with regional consumer protection law:

Regional consumer protection law always takes precedence
ShopCore standard policy applies where no conflict exists
Seller-specific policy applies for marketplace orders (but cannot undercut regional law)

5.2 Agent Escalation for Regional Conflicts
Agents encountering regional law questions outside of documented regions above should:

Escalate to the Legal & Compliance team
Not make autonomous policy determinations
Issue a holding response to the customer while escalation is in progress


Section 6: Marketplace Seller Regional Obligations
6.1 Seller Compliance Requirements
Marketplace sellers operating in regulated regions (EU, UK, CA, Quebec) must comply with applicable regional consumer protection laws. Sellers who fail to comply may be:

Required by ShopCore to honor regional consumer rights
Subject to A-to-Z Guarantee claims even if outside their stated policy

6.2 Cross-Border Marketplace Disputes
For disputes involving an international customer and a domestic seller (or vice versa):

The law of the customer's jurisdiction applies for consumer rights purposes
Seller is responsible for understanding the regulations of regions they sell into
ShopCore mediates cross-border disputes under its A-to-Z Guarantee
